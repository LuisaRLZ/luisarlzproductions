About Topperf.exe

Download Topperf.exe

  Folders to backup before installing: texture, effect and shader 

Topperf.exe is an executable file that you may put inside the main folder of your client's folder. Once you double click it, you will be given multiple options on which effects to delete, including wings, weapons, etc.

However it is always recommended to backup your files, as topperf.exe can be a pretty... aggresive, let's call it, tweak. It is a common mistake to delete way too many effects to the point it is not even possible to see attack damage and other very necessary effects.

The point of this tweak is to allow your PC to relax a bit without having it render so much stuff at the same time, making it at least, considerably, less laggy.

Nowadays it doesn't hold as much impact as it did years ago, as modern PCs can withstand a lot more than the PCs 13 years ago, but if you happen to have an older PC, this tweak might be relevant for you.
This tweak is one of the oldest existing ones, dating even before I began playing this game, probably around late 2007. Back then PCs were very weak, you know, the cool ancient museum-worthy artifacts with as little as 64mb RAM and the times youtube was actually cool. So, back then, this tweak was very relevant for a lot of people, as it made the difference between being able to play at all or crash every 3 minutes (also back then, the game would take like 10 minutes to open... dear god).

Nowadays it is nonwhere near as relevant as 15 years ago (wow it's been 15 years already? damn we are old), but it is still a precious file to keep around, and might actually come of some use for some people.

The cool thing is that thanks to topperf.exe nowadays most modern servers have options to activate and deactivate effects already in-game, such a QoL change!

The most common problem is to overdelete effects to the point the game becomes unplayable. To fix it I recommend:

1. To backup your whole effects, texture and shader folders before running topperf.exe. In the case you delete too much, just put those 3 folders back into your game's client and try again.

2. In the case you did not back up anything, run 'Version Check' in your game's launcher. This, however, will most likely remove any installed tweak you may have.

In the case you're unable to install it or it seems it's not working...

1. Make sure you're putting this tweak in your client's folder.

2. Make sure you have writing permissions in your client's folder (You can do this by right clicking any empty space in the client's folder then check all user permissions).

3. Make sure... you're opening the correct client to begin with. It has happened to me that, while having several different game clients installed in my PC, sometimes I get frustrated opening a different client than the one I installed the tweak at...
